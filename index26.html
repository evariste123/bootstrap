1. Our Mission

EduHub is a decentralized platform designed to bridge the digital divide. We provide standardized HTML templates that work on any browser, in any country, regardless of internet speed. By mastering these snippets, users can create professional documentation, resumes, and business websites.

2. The Foundation (Boilerplate)

Every professional web document requires a <head> for metadata and a <body> for visible content.

<!-- The Document Type Declaration -->
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
  <head>
    <meta charset="UTF-8">
    <title>Page Title</title>
  </head>
  <body>
    <!-- Content goes here -->
  </body>
</html>

3. Visuals & Lists

Use these codes to organize information into bullet points or to display images and videos.

Unordered List (Bullet Points)

<ul>
  <li>First item</li>
  <li>Second item</li>
</ul>

Images with Accessibility

The alt attribute is essential for global accessibility (screen readers).

<img src="image-url.jpg" alt="Description of the image" width="300">

4. Information Tables

Tables are the best way to display structured data like schedules, pricing, or inventory.

<table border="1">
  <tr>
    <th>Service</th>
    <th>Price</th>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Web Design</td>
    <td>$0 (Open Source)</td>
  </tr>
</table>

5. User Interactivity (Forms)

Forms allow your global audience to communicate with you.

<form action="/submit-path" method="POST">
  <label>Choose Category:</label>
  <select name="category">
    <option value="beginner">Beginner</option>
    <option value="expert">Expert</option>
  </select>
  <button type="submit">Apply Filter</button>
</form>
